Kitchen​, ​cabinet​s, and Baking​Comm

​

Kitchen rules:

  • We have communal pots, pans, dishes, silverware, etc.
  • Please clean dishes within a reasonable time after using them.
  • All dishes that go on the drying rack (between the two right sinks) are assumed communal, so dry your personal dishes elsewhere.
  • Dish soap in squirt bottle and sponges provided, but sometimes we run out.
  • Cabinets are first-come first-serve. Claim by putting your name on the front.
  • We have had issues in the past with gross sinks building up, etc. Each semester we've tried a different system. We're unsure what the system will be in the spring, but it'll be discussed at the hall meeting.

We have a BakingComm that buys communal flour, sugar, etc. You can use these items ONLY IF you serve your final dish to hall. You don't have to wash the dishes you used but you should coerce the people who ate your food to clean a dish or two.

​

Bathrooms and Trash​

​Bathrooms are all co-ed. The shallow Goodale bathroom is single-occupancy. Please knock before entering to prevent double-awkupancy.​
Throw away large items or your personal bag o' trash in the trash chutes located in the janitor closets in Goodale and Walcott. When hall trash bins get too full, tie up the bag and throw it down the chute. Extra bags are inside the bins.
Evelina cleans our hall every non-holiday weekday. Please be considerate with you trash output and stuff. Also if you leave anything out on hall and looks like trash (even dragonstaffs are trash in her eyes) it'll probably be grunged by Evelina.

Painting and Murals

See our mural policy here.

In general:

  • whitespace is free to paint
  • approved murals need a petition to paint over
  • unapproved murals can be painted over
  • door and the inside of your room are free to paint by the resident
  • a petition needs 20 (?) signatures and needs to be posted near the site of the mural. if it doesn't reach the quota within 4 (?) weeks it fails (this never happens.) For IAP/summer projects, signatures can be gathered via email to beast@
  • However some people have feelings about door murals, so you can email out but expect to hear some upset opinions.
